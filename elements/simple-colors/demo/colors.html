Colors

By default, simple-colors has the following 18 base colors: grey, red, pink, purple, deep-purple, indigo, blue, light-blue, cyan, teal, green, light-green, lime, yellow, amber, orange, brown, and blue-grey. Each color has 12 shades.

You can choose a specifc color or you can set let the color be determined contextually based on a custom-element's accent-color property.

The shades of each color are organized by theme. If you use the lowest numbered shades for background and the highest numbered shades for foreground, the colors will fit each theme.

simple-colors-default-theme

The default theme arranges the shades based on wether or not a custom-element has the dark property set. Normally --simple-colors-default-theme-red-1 would be the lightest shade of red and --simple-colors-default-theme-red-12 the darkest. If the dark attribute attribute is set, the shades reverse so that --simple-colors-default-theme-red-1 is the darkest and --simple-colors-default-theme-red-12 is the lightest. These shading becomes inverted without having to recode every color variable.

simple-colors-light-theme

The light theme always arranges the shades from lightest to darkest. So --simple-colors-default-theme-red-1 would be the lightest shade of red and --simple-colors-default-theme-red-12 the darkest.

simple-colors-dark-theme

The dark theme always arranges the shades from darkest to lightest. So --simple-colors-default-theme-red-1 would be the darkest shade of red and --simple-colors-default-theme-red-12 the lightest.
