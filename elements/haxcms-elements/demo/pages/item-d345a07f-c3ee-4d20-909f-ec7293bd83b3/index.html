Accessibility means that HAX will always strive to maximize accessibility while simultaneously removing the knowledge required to maintain those accessibility standards. HAX themes and elements produced are highly accessible and when accessibility issues arise in our ecosystem we address them rapidly. The HAX editor seeks to maximize its accessibility and a lot of time has gone into ensuring UIs are accessible, though we are always open to recommendations on how to improve the UX for all abilities of users.

The pillar in action

  • The "Simple Colors" library we wrote automatically ensures color contrast ratios between high and low tones to guarantee accessibility is kept to WCAG 2.0 AA thresholds as users implement color selection in elements using this library (which is many)
  • Multiple universities and government agents have audited previous editions of HAX to provide expert recommendations on how the community should build solutions
  • We highly prioritize accessibility issues given our client base and commitment to all users, 'ability' agnostic
  • Elements engineered because of this effort are not limited to but include:
    • GIF player
    • Video player
    • Learning Component
